# Design-rule novelty check

**The rule under test**: layered host + donor ion + interlayer (gallery) spacing tuned to the ion's bistability threshold. Opening the gallery past a critical spacing simultaneously (i) creates an interlayer 2D electron gas (the donor's electrons partially return to the gallery) and (ii) softens the intercalant ion into an anharmonic double-well / quantum-paraelectric regime; superconductivity occupies only the window where carriers exist AND the ion mode is soft; too-open galleries freeze the ion (polaronic, SC killed).

All citations below were verified against publisher/arXiv records (verification pass 2026-07-12); items that could not be fully verified are flagged in §6.

---

## (a) Quantum-paraelectric / soft-polar-mode-boundary superconductivity (SrTiO3 program)

- **Edge, Kedem, Aschauer, Spaldin, Balatsky, PRL 115, 247002 (2015)** (+ Erratum PRL 117, 219901 (2016); arXiv:1507.08275): the SrTiO3 dome peaks where the ferroelectric soft-TO mode crosses zero frequency; "soft mode fluctuations provide the pairing interaction"; predicted 18O-enhanced Tc.
- **Stucky et al., Sci. Rep. 6, 37582 (2016)** (van der Marel group): giant *inverse* oxygen isotope effect — 18O substitution (pushing toward the FE instability) raises Tc ~50%. Direct experimental support for soft-polar-mode pairing.
- **Rowley et al., Nature Physics 10, 367 (2014)**: SrTiO3/KTaO3 established as ferroelectric quantum-critical materials (1/χ ∝ T²).
- **Rischau et al., Nature Physics 13, 643 (2017)**: Ca-tuned FE quantum phase transition *inside* the SC dome; coexistence and enhancement.
- **Gastiasoro, Ruhman, Fernandes, Annals of Physics 417, 168107 (2020)**: authoritative review; notes the theoretical difficulty (TO mode has no linear density coupling in a single band — needs SOC-assisted or two-phonon coupling) while accepting the phenomenology.
- **Generalizations**: strain-enhanced Tc in films (Ahadi et al., Sci. Adv. 5, eaaw0120 (2019)); KTaO3(111) interface SC (Liu et al., Science 371, 716 (2021)) with soft-TO-phonon pairing proposals (Liu et al., Nat. Commun. 14, 951 (2023)); "quantum critical polar metals" (Volkov, Chandra, Coleman, Nat. Commun. 13, 4599 (2022)); ferroelectric-SC theory (Kanasugi & Yanase, PRB 98, 024521 (2018); PRB 100, 094504 (2019)); Enderlein et al., Nat. Commun. 11, 4852 (2020).

**Proximity**: the "tune to the polar quantum critical point" logic is fully developed — but always for a *host lattice* soft mode in bulk perovskites (or their interface 2DEGs), never for the *donor ion itself*, never in an intercalate, and never with spacing as the knob. The carriers and the soft mode have independent origins (chemical doping vs host lattice); in our rule one structural parameter controls both.

## (b) Interlayer / gallery 2DEG states in layered materials

- **Csányi, Littlewood, Nevidomskyy, Pickard, Simons, Nature Physics 1, 42 (2005)** (cond-mat/0503569): in graphite intercalation compounds, "in all those — and only those — compounds that superconduct ... an interlayer state ... becomes occupied"; and, critically for us: "the energy of the interlayer band is controlled by a combination of its occupancy and the separation between the carbon layers." Proposes excitonic/acoustic-plasmon pairing from the interlayer electrons.
- **Calandra & Mauri, PRL 95, 237002 (2005)**: the conventional counterpoint for CaC6 — λ = 0.83 from Ca in-plane + C out-of-plane phonons coupled to interlayer/Ca-derived Fermi surface. **Mazin, PRL 95, 227001 (2005)**: ~85% of coupling from intercalant (Ca/Yb) modes; SC requires intercalant-derived states at E_F *and* "sizable coupling with soft intercalant modes."
- **Profeta, Calandra, Mauri, Nature Physics 8, 131 (2012)**: LiC6 monolayer — explicit design condition "bring the interlayer state at the Fermi level"; confirmed by Ichinokura et al., ACS Nano 10, 2761 (2016) (C6CaC6 superconducts, C6LiC6 does not).
- **Electrides**: Lee, Kim, Toda, Matsuishi, Hosono, Nature 494, 336 (2013) — Ca2N = [Ca2N]+·e−, a literal gallery-confined 2D anionic electron gas; SC in the electride C12A7:e− at 0.14–0.4 K (Miyakawa et al., JACS 129, 7270 (2007)); predicted electride SC (Li6P, PRL 122, 097002 (2019); Y2C monolayer, NJP 19 (2017), flagged). No experimental Ca2N superconductivity.
- **NaxCoO2·yH2O itself**: no gallery-electron proposal exists. Johannes & Singh, PRB 70, 014507 (2004) is an explicit *negative* DFT result — water acts as a spacer with "little effect ... outside of the predictable effects of expansion," no interlayer states at E_F. The pairing literature is spin/charge-fluctuation dominated (Mazin & Johannes, Nature Physics 1, 91 (2005)).

**Proximity**: the interlayer-state-occupancy criterion for SC exists (GICs), and even the statement that layer separation controls it (Csányi). What does not exist: the identification of the *donor ion's own returned charge* as the gallery gas in an ionic intercalate, or any coupling of that gas to donor-ion anharmonicity. Note the direct risk: for our flagship material, the standard DFT literature denies gallery states at E_F — our proposal must confront Johannes–Singh head-on (their calculation is for the static, symmetric ice-like water configuration; our claim rests on the bifurcated/displaced ion configurations).

## (c) Rattler / anharmonic-ion superconductivity

- **β-pyrochlore KOs2O6**: Tc = 9.6 K (Yonezawa, Muraoka, Matsushita, Hiroi, J. Phys.: Condens. Matter 16, L9 (2004)). The K+ rattles in an oversized cage with an extremely flat, quartic-dominated potential (Kuneš, Jeong, Pickett, PRB 70, 174510 (2004): unstable optic mode, ~0.5–0.6 Å ⟨111⟩ excursions; giant ADPs, Yamaura et al., JSSC 179, 336 (2006)). Smaller ion → flatter well → stronger anharmonic coupling → higher Tc (Cs 3.3 K → Rb 6.3 K → K 9.6 K): Hiroi, Yamaura, Hattori, "Rattling Good Superconductor," JPSJ 81, 011012 (2012). Theory: Hattori & Tsunetsugu, PRB 81, 134503 (2010) — anharmonic-phonon-mediated strong-coupling SC with quantum-tunneling enhancement; Dahm & Ueda, PRL 99, 187003 (2007); Nagao et al., JPSJ 78, 064702 (2009). *Language caution*: the consensus wording is "extremely flat, strongly anharmonic, marginally off-center" — no primary source flatly calls K in KOs2O6 a "double well."
- **Clathrates**: Ba8Si46, Tc = 8.0 K (Yamanaka et al., Inorg. Chem. 39, 56 (2000)); anharmonic Ba modes visible in α²F (Lortz et al., PRB 77, 224507 (2008)).
- **Valence-space bistability**: Tl-doped PbTe — charge-Kondo/negative-U pairing from Tl1+/Tl3+ valence skipping (Matsushita, Bluhm, Geballe, Fisher, PRL 94, 157002 (2005); Dzero & Schmalian, PRL 94, 157003 (2005)). Bistable in *charge*, not position.
- **Hydrides (brief)**: SSCHA anharmonicity is decisive in H3S (Errea, Calandra, Mauri, PRL 114, 157004 (2015)); Errea et al., Nature 532, 81 (2016) is a literal quantum double-well-to-single-well (H-bond symmetrization) crossover controlling SC — under megabar pressure in a 3D hydride.

**Proximity**: "soft anharmonic ion mode as pairing glue" is established (KOs2O6 is the anchor). But every rattler system is a *3D cage* compound: the ion's environment is fixed by cage chemistry, the knobs are ion size and hydrostatic pressure, the carriers are host-band (Os 5d) — no 2DEG, no spacing, no on/off carrier switch, and the rattler never *donates* the carriers it pairs.

## (d) The combination — closest prior art

1. **CaC6 cluster: Calandra & Mauri PRL 95, 237002 + Mazin PRL 95, 227001 + Csányi et al. Nat. Phys. 1, 42 (2005).** Already contains, in one material family: occupied interlayer band as the SC criterion, layer separation controlling that band, and pairing dominated by soft *intercalant* phonons coupled to interlayer electrons. This is ~60% of our rule. Missing: any anharmonicity/bistability of the intercalant (Ca phonons treated harmonically), any quantum-paraelectric framing, any SC-death by ion freezing, and any closed window in spacing.
2. **Gauzzi et al., PRL 98, 067002 (2007)** — CaC6 under pressure: Tc rises 11.5 → 15.1 K as the in-plane Ca mode *softens*, then a structural instability at ~8 GPa collapses Tc to ~5 K. Empirically this is exactly our motif — soft intercalant mode enhances Tc until the ion orders and SC dies — but with pressure (not gallery opening) as the knob and purely harmonic-BCS interpretation.
3. **Shenoy, Subrahmanyam, Bishop, PRL 79, 4657 (1997)**, "Quantum Paraelectric Model for Layered Superconductors": a 2D carrier layer coupled to an adjacent layer of anharmonic quantum-paraelectric dipoles; mode exchange yields local d-wave-like pairing with Tc maxima. The closest *conceptual* ancestor of "carriers in one layer, quantum-paraelectric units in the next" — aimed at cuprate oxygens, with no intercalant ion, no gallery 2DEG, no spacing knob, and (per citation record) no experimental follow-up.

Also adjacent, for completeness: **Kasahara et al., PRB 82, 054504 (2010)** — the cleanest prior use of interlayer spacing as an isolated SC tuning knob (β-ZrNCl; see `ZRNCL.md`: monotonic, intercalant demonstrably uninvolved — mechanistically the opposite of ours); **Biswas, Sutradhar, Saha, Klein, Ruhman, arXiv:2601.03352 (Jan 2026)** — interlayer charge-transfer ferroelectric fluctuations as pairing glue in vdW superconductors (host interlayer phonon near an FE QCP; no intercalant, no gallery gas, no spacing threshold) — evidence the "polar quantum criticality meets vdW gap" area is active *right now*; and the NIST neutron work on the hydrate (Lynn et al., PRB 68, 214516 (2003)), whose public summary speculates only loosely that "anharmonic motion of the hydrogen and oxygen ions might be playing a role" — the nearest the hydrate literature ever came to our ingredient (ii), never developed.

## 5. Verdict

**What is repackaging.** Each ingredient, taken alone, is established prior art and must be cited as such: soft-polar-mode/quantum-critical pairing (SrTiO3 program, 2014–2022); occupied interlayer states as the switch for SC in intercalates, including their spacing dependence (Csányi 2005; Profeta 2012); anharmonic/rattling-ion pairing with flat single-ion potentials (KOs2O6, 2004–2012); soft-intercalant-mode enhancement followed by freeze-out collapse (Gauzzi 2007, in pressure space); carriers-next-to-quantum-paraelectric-layer pairing as an abstract model (Shenoy 1997). Presenting any of these as new would be indefensible.

**What is genuinely new** (nothing found in three independent search passes): (1) making the **donor ion itself** the quantum-paraelectric unit — in all prior art the soft/bistable mode belongs to the host lattice or a caged spectator, never to the species that donates the carriers; (2) the claim that **one structural knob — gallery spacing — simultaneously switches on the carrier reservoir (gallery 2DEG of the ion's own returned electrons) and tunes the ion through its bistability threshold**, mechanically entangling ingredients that are independent in every predecessor; (3) the resulting **two-sided window**: SC bounded below by carrier onset and above by polaronic freeze-out — prior art has one-sided versions only (Csányi: carrier onset; Gauzzi: freeze-out; SrTiO3: dome in *doping*, not in a structural parameter); (4) applying any of this to NaxCoO2·yH2O, where the literature treats water/Na strictly as spacer + electrostatic dopant. The main vulnerability is not priority but *truth*: Johannes & Singh (PRB 70, 014507 (2004)) find no gallery states at E_F in the static hydrate structure, so the paper must show explicitly how ion displacement/bifurcation changes that conclusion.

**Sharpest defensible one-sentence claim:**

> We propose the first superconductivity design rule in which the donor ion of a layered intercalate is simultaneously the carrier source and the pairing boson: opening the gallery past a critical spacing both releases the ion's donated electrons into an interlayer 2D electron gas and tunes the ion itself to a quantum-paraelectric bistability threshold, so that superconductivity occupies a closed window in spacing — bounded below by carrier onset and above by polaronic freeze-out — a combination absent from the SrTiO3 quantum-critical, graphite-interlayer-state, and rattler-superconductor literatures.

If a referee forces a one-clause version: *spacing as the single knob that co-tunes a gallery 2DEG and the donor ion's own bistability is new; every ingredient separately is not.*

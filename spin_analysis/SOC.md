# SOC.md — is spin–orbit coupling first-order relevant to the gallery 2DEG?

Short answer: **no for pairing-symmetry selection, marginal for the spin response.**
Numbers below; every assumed quantity is tagged ansatz with a range.

## 1. Atomic SOC scales in the gallery band

| Ion | SOC scale | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Co 3d | λ ≈ 70 meV (given) | only enters via the small Co-d admixture in the gallery band |
| O 2p | tens of meV | small O weight in the gallery state |
| Na 3s/3p | ~2 meV (3p, Na D-line splitting 2.1 meV; 3s carries none on-site) | negligible (given) |

The gallery state is Na sp_z-dominated (R&L: surface state "hovering slightly above"
the alkali). Its Co-d weight is bounded by the SSH4 interlayer mixing: amplitude
~ (τ1/τ4) = t_Li^z / t_Li–CoO2 = 0.5/2.0 (paper values), i.e. weight ~ (0.25)² ≈ 0.06,
of which roughly half is actually Co d inside the CoO2 Wannier orbital →
**f_d ≈ 0.03 (ansatz range 0.01–0.1)**. Effective on-band SOC: f_d·λ_Co ≈ 2 meV.

## 2. Rashba term from off-center Na (local inversion breaking)

When Na sits in one minimum of the double well (displacement δ_z ≈ 0.15–0.5 Å — the
runpod grid's δ values; ansatz for the physical value pending the E(δ) scans), the 2DEG
feels an asymmetric environment → Rashba term α_R (σ × k)·ẑ.

**Field estimate** (dimensional analysis, per the tasking: Bader charge shift over the
well displacement): the asymmetric charge is ΔQ ≈ 0.4 e (paper's alkali Bader charge;
ansatz that the full shifted charge participates) displaced by δ_z ≈ 0.3 Å at distance
r ≈ 2 Å from the 2DEG centroid, screened by ε ≈ 4 (gallery water/oxide, ansatz 2–8):

    E_z ≈ (14.4 V·Å · ΔQ / ε) · [1/(r−δ_z/2)² − 1/(r+δ_z/2)²] ≈ 0.1–0.5 V/Å

**Route 1 — bare field (vacuum Rashba):** α = (ħ²/4m²c²)·eE_z = 3.7×10⁻⁶ Ų × eE_z
≈ 10⁻⁶ eV·Å. Utterly negligible — the field alone does nothing; SOC needs atoms.

**Route 2 — atomic-SOC amplification (the real channel):**

    α_R ~ 2 · f_d · λ_Co · (δ_z / w_z) · a
        ~ 2 × 0.03 × 70 meV × (0.3 Å / 2 Å) × 2.84 Å ≈ **2 meV·Å**
    (ansatz ranges compound to ~0.2–8 meV·Å)

with w_z ≈ 2 Å the vertical extent of the gallery state (ansatz) and a the in-plane
lattice constant.

## 3. Magnitude relative to the gap

Gallery Fermi surface at the baseline operating point (from `stoner_model.py`,
q = 0.12, x = 0.35): n₂DEG ≈ 6×10¹³ cm⁻², k_F ≈ 0.20 Å⁻¹, m* ≈ 1.2 m_e, E_F ≈ 0.12 eV.
Gap scale: Δ ≈ 1.76 k_B Tc = 0.68 meV (Tc = 4.5 K, weak-coupling conversion).

| Quantity | Baseline | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Rashba band splitting at k_F, E_R = 2·α_R·k_F | 0.8 meV | 0.08–3.2 meV |
| E_R / Δ | ~1 | 0.1–5 |
| E_R / E_F | 0.007 | <0.03 |

**Parity mixing of the pairing:** the singlet–triplet admixture generated by Rashba
splitting is controlled by E_R/E_F ≈ 0.3% (helicity-band picture valid only if
E_R ≫ Δ, which is not even reached at baseline). **The induced parity-mixed component
of the gap is at the sub-percent level.**

**Global-symmetry suppression:** the average bilayer-hydrate structure is
centrosymmetric; the Na off-centering is up/down disordered or staggered between
galleries. Opposite sectors carry opposite α_R ("hidden Rashba"), so macroscopic
Rashba effects cancel to the extent the two sectors are coupled — a further suppression
on top of the smallness above.

## 4. Verdict

- **Not first-order** for the pairing problem: SOC cannot select or mix pairing
  channels at more than the % level; the Mazin–Johannes singlet/f-wave competition is
  decided by paramagnons and phonons (SPIN.md), not SOC.
- **Marginally relevant for spin-response observables**: E_R is of order Δ, so
  spin-momentum locking can leave a *residual Knight shift / unsuppressed χ_spin
  fraction of order (E_R/Δ)²-ish weight at T→0* in a singlet state — a second,
  SOC-based contribution (besides paramagnon proximity, SPIN.md §4) to the historical
  singlet-vs-triplet NMR confusion. μSR TRS-null unaffected (Rashba breaks inversion
  locally, not time reversal).

## 5. The one cheap DFT check

One calculation settles α_R: take the existing runpod geometry at **c = 9.9 Å,
δ = 0.5 Å** (job `Na_1x1_c9.9_d0.50`), switch to fully-relativistic pseudopotentials
(pslibrary `*rel-pbe*kjpaw` for Co/O/Na), `noncolin = .true., lspinorb = .true.`,
one SCF + a band run along Γ→M and Γ→K up to k∥ ≈ 0.3 Å⁻¹, and fit the gallery-band
spin splitting ΔE(k∥) = 2 α_R k∥ below 0.15 Å⁻¹ (identify the band by its Na-layer
projection, as in the existing postprocess). Single job, well under one GPU-hour at
the batch's 60 Ry settings. Decision rule: **α_R < 10 meV·Å confirms "not first-order"
(expected); α_R ≳ 30 meV·Å would force SOC into the pairing discussion** and predict a
measurable in-plane-field anisotropy of H_c2.
